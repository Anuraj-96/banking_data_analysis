# banking_data_analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes deposit behavior and client financial profiles in a banking dataset containing 3,000 customers. The goal is to visualize key patterns and trends to support decision making across products, customer segments and bank operations.


### Business Problem

Banks often struggle with:
Underutilized high-value customers
Low product penetration
Inefficient customer segmentation
Missed upsell and upgrade opportunities 

This project addresses these challenges by identifying:
Who the valuable customers are
Where revenue is being lost
How to increase customer lifetime value


### Focus on:

Customer demographics & financial behavior
Cohort-based growth trends
Churn risk segmentation
Product usage patterns
Upsell & cross sell opportunities

### Key Insigths

Customer base is skewed towards older age groups (55+)
Gender distribution is balanced (~50/50)
Majority customers belong to mid to high income segments
Significant growth observed post-2005

Churn Risk Distribution : 
Very Low Risk → 40.7% 
Medium to High Risk → ~31%

There is a strong correlation between loyalty tier and revenue contribution.
Jade customers contribute the highest share (~$927M)

Medium income customers contribute $1.09B in deposits

Increasing number of cards does not significantly increase spending

Low adoption of forex services = major cross-sell opportunity
